## What Is App Factory?

App Factory is KITLabs' public-facing interactive platform that takes customers from **"I have an app idea"** to **"my app is live in the App Store"** — all in one guided, AI-powered experience.

Unlike traditional app development where customers describe an idea on a call, wait weeks for wireframes, and go back-and-forth over email — App Factory lets them **see their app come to life in minutes**, right in their browser.

**One-liner:** *Describe your app idea. Watch AI design it. Click through it like a real app. Submit it for building.*

**Tagline options for marketing:**
- "Turn your app idea into reality"
- "From idea to app — powered by AI"
- "See your app before it's built"
- "Your app, designed in minutes — built by experts"

## The Customer Journey (4 Phases)

### Phase 1: Ideate 💡
**What the customer does:** Types their app idea in plain language.

**What happens:**
- Customer lands on appfactory.kitlabs.us and clicks "Start Building"
- Types their app concept — can be as simple as "I want an app for booking dog walkers"
- Selects target platforms: iOS, Android, and/or Web
- Signs in with Google, LinkedIn, or email/password (free, no credit card)

**Marketing angle:** *"No technical knowledge needed. Just describe your idea like you'd tell a friend."*

**Helpful prompts built in:**
- 20 industry-specific starter prompts mapped to NAICS sectors (Healthcare, Fintech, Real Estate, Education, etc.)
- 6 portfolio example cards from real KITLabs apps with pre-written prompts customers can use as starting points (KITFit, Overdims, Squabbler, Care Partners, My Digital Card, Whenworx)

---

### Phase 2: Design 🎨
**What the customer does:** Reviews AI-generated screens and requirements, then refines them through conversation.

**Two views — customer can toggle between both:**

#### Visual View
- AI generates **mobile app screen mockups** with realistic UI elements (buttons, inputs, navigation bars, cards, lists, maps, etc.)
- Screens render as **phone-frame cards** with status bar, elements, and home indicator
- Customer sees all screens in a grid, or views the **flow diagram** showing how screens connect
- **Click any screen** to open a detail view with:
  - Large phone preview
  - Editable screen name
  - Notes section
  - Linked requirements
  - Per-screen AI chat ("Make this screen simpler")

#### Requirements View
- AI generates a **structured Scope of Work** organized by feature
- Each feature includes: name, description, linked screens, acceptance criteria, priority (P0/P1/P2), estimated complexity
- Tech stack recommendations and timeline estimate
- Customer can ask the AI to add, remove, or modify features

#### Interactive Prototype Preview ▶️
- Customer clicks the **"Preview" tab** to walk through their app like a real prototype
- **Click buttons and cards** to navigate between screens (slide transitions)
- **Back button** with history stack
- **Screen navigator dots** at the bottom for jumping to any screen
- **Shareable link** — customer can copy the preview URL and send to anyone (no login required to view)

**Marketing angle:** *"See your app come to life before a single line of code is written. Click through screens like a real app. Share with your team for instant feedback."*

#### 8 Color Themes
Customer can pick from 8 color themes and instantly see all screens recolor:
- KITLabs Blue (default)
- Ocean
- Forest
- Sunset
- Royal Purple
- Midnight
- Rose
- Teal

**Marketing angle:** *"Choose your brand colors and watch every screen update instantly."*

---

### Phase 3: Build 🔨
**What the customer does:** Submits finalized design for professional development.

**What happens:**
- Customer clicks "Finalize Design" → "Submit for Build"
- Optionally provides company name
- Configures **App Store accounts** (can do now or later):
  - **Apple App Store**: Team ID, Bundle ID, API Key
  - **Google Play Store**: Developer Account ID, Package Name, Service Account Key
- A **Lead is automatically created** in the KITLabs admin system with full requirements attached
- Customer sees a **6-step build status tracker**: Submitted → In Review → Building → Testing → Ready → Delivered

**Email notifications at every step:**
- "Your app is being reviewed"
- "Development has started!"
- "Your app is in testing"
- "Your app build is ready for review!"
- "🎉 Your app has been delivered!"

**Marketing angle:** *"One click to submit. We handle the rest. Track progress in real-time — from code to app store."*

---

### Phase 4: Enhance ✨
**What the customer does:** Requests changes and improvements after delivery.

**What happens:**
- Customer describes what they want changed ("Add push notifications", "Change the dashboard layout")
- **AI analyzes the impact** and shows:
  - Which existing screens will be modified
  - What new screens are needed
  - Which features are affected
  - New features that would be added
  - Estimated effort (Low / Medium / High)
  - Risks and considerations
- Customer reviews the analysis and submits the enhancement
- Same status tracking: Requested → Reviewed → Approved → Building → Delivered

**Marketing angle:** *"Your app grows with your business. Request changes anytime — our AI shows you the impact before we start building."*

## Customer Account Features

### My Projects
- Dashboard showing all projects with status, build progress, and creation date
- Each project card shows a mini progress bar (6 dots) for build tracking
- "New Project" button to start additional apps

### Profile
- Profile picture upload
- Name, phone, company name
- Member since date

### Inbox (Notifications)
- All lifecycle notifications in one place
- Color-coded by type:
  - 🔨 Build updates (orange)
  - 🎉 Build delivered (green)
  - ✨ Enhancement updates (purple)
  - 👀 Review ready (blue)
  - 👋 Welcome (teal)
- Read/unread status with badge count in nav bar
- Click any notification to jump to the relevant project page

### Authentication
- **Google Sign-In** (one click)
- **LinkedIn Sign-In** (one click)
- **Email/Password** (traditional)
- Same account works on both App Factory and the Customer Portal

## Key Differentiators for Marketing

### vs. Traditional App Development
| Traditional | App Factory |
|---|---|
| Weeks of back-and-forth calls | 5 minutes to see your app designed |
| Static wireframes in PDF | Interactive prototype you can click through |
| Scope of Work via email | Structured requirements with acceptance criteria |

|---|---|
| You build it yourself | We build it for you |

| Steep learning curve | Just describe your idea |
| Ongoing maintenance is yours | Full-service — we maintain it |

### vs. Figma / Design Tools
| Design Tools | App Factory |
|---|---|
| You need a designer | AI designs it from your description |
| Static mockups | Interactive clickable prototype |
| Design ≠ development | Design → build → deploy, all connected |

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Non-technical founders, small business owners, entrepreneurs who have an app idea but don't know where to start.

**Secondary:** Product managers at mid-size companies who want to quickly prototype and validate ideas before committing to full development.

**Tertiary:** Agencies and consultants who want to present app concepts to their clients with interactive prototypes.

**Industries with highest potential** (based on our NAICS prompt categories):
- Healthcare (patient apps, telehealth, appointment booking)
- Finance (fintech, budgeting, payment apps)
- Real Estate (property management, listings, tenant apps)
- Food & Hospitality (ordering, delivery, reservation apps)
- Education (e-learning, tutoring, student management)
- Professional Services (booking, CRM, client portals)
- Retail & E-Commerce (storefronts, inventory, loyalty programs)

## Pricing Notes

App Factory itself is **free to use** — no cost to:
- Create an account
- Describe your idea
- Generate screens and requirements
- Refine the design with AI
- Preview and share the prototype
- Submit for build

**Revenue model:** KITLabs charges for the professional app development after the customer submits for build. The pricing is custom-quoted based on the scope of work generated during the design phase.

**Marketing angle:** *"Design your app for free. Only pay when you're ready to build."*
